# ☕ Customer Experience Differences Between To-Go and Sit-Down Specialty Cafés

## 📌 About the Project
In this project, I analyzed customer reviews to better understand what influences satisfaction in specialty cafes. I focused on how customer experience differs between to-go locations and sit-down cafes.

## 🎯 What I Wanted to Find Out
- What factors matter most for customer satisfaction  
- How expectations change depending on the cafe format  
- What kind of insights review sentiment and topics can reveal  

## 🛠 Tools & Technologies
- Python  
- Pandas  
- Text processing (NLP basics)  
- Data analysis and visualization  

## 📊 Main Findings
- Coffee quality is the strongest driver of positive reviews across both café formats, showing that the core product has the biggest impact on customer satisfaction.
- Staff behavior is the most common source of negative feedback, which suggests that customer interaction plays a critical role in how the overall experience is perceived.
- Negative reviews often mention multiple issues at the same time (for example service, product quality, and atmosphere), while positive reviews usually focus on one main strength.
- Sit-down cafés tend to receive slightly lower ratings because customers evaluate the entire experience, not just the product. Service quality, atmosphere, and comfort all influence satisfaction.
- To-go cafés generally receive higher ratings, likely because visits are shorter and expectations focus mainly on speed and product consistency.
- Customer expectations clearly differ depending on the visit type: to-go experiences are more functional, while sit-down visits are more experiential.
- Operational factors such as cleanliness, food quality, and service speed become important mostly when something goes wrong, rather than being actively praised.
